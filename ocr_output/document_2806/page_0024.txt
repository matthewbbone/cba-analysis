ARTICLE III. PAY, HOURS AND BENEFITS

Section 1. Wages.

A. General Wage Increase for 2003-2004

137. Employees shall not receive a general wage increase in fiscal year 2003-2004.

B. General Wage Increases for Fiscal Years 2004-2005, 2005-2006 and 2006-2007

138. 1. On or about February 15th, 2004 and on or about that date each year thereafter for the duration of this Agreement, the City shall conduct a salary survey of the rank of Police Officer (Q-2) in the following California cities:

• Oakland
• San Jose
• Los Angeles
• Santa Rosa
• Fremont
• Richmond

139. These jurisdictions have been selected because of their comparable size and/or their proximity to San Francisco and because of the similarity of the police services provided. The police agencies in the jurisdictions used in this survey utilize work week schedules similar to those used in the San Francisco Police Department.

140. 2. The salary survey shall measure total compensation based on the following data points:

• Maximum monthly salary for the rank of police officer (Q2);
• Longevity pay;
• Uniform pay/allowance;
• Educational incentives (e.g., POST);
• Employer payment of mandatory employee retirement contributions and retirement supplements;
• Maximum monthly total employer contribution for the following insurance benefits: health, dental, vision, LTD and life insurance.
