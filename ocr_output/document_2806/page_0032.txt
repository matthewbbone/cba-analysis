194. Employees called back to work to perform their regular duties during the period of on-call service shall be paid while engaged in such service at the usual rate of pay as provided herein. (However, on-call pay shall not be allowed in the classes or positions whose duties are primarily administrative in nature, as designated by the Chief of the Department.) On-Call Premiums shall not be included for purposes of retirement benefit calculations or contributions.

H. Night Shift Differential

195. Night shift differential shall be paid at the rate of six and one-quarter percent (6-1/4%) more than the base rate for hours actually worked between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. This night differential shall not be included for purposes of retirement benefit calculations or contributions.

I. Bilingual Pay

196. Bilingual pay, in the amount of $35.00 biweekly, shall be paid to employees who have been certified by the Department of Human Resources as having proficiency in the Spanish or Chinese languages. Employees certified by the Department of Human Resources as having proficiency in other languages, including sign language for the hearing impaired and braille for the visually impaired, shall, upon the approval of their supervisor, receive such pay when they are required to utilize such skills, and subject to Department of Human Resources guidelines. Bilingual Pay shall not be included for purposes of retirement benefit calculations or contributions.

J. Terrorism Response Training Pay

197. Effective July 1, 2004, employees shall receive a one percent (1%) adjustment to their regular rate of pay in recognition of mandated, specialized training dealing with emergencies involving terrorism and bio-terrorism. Terrorism Response Training Pay shall be considered as part of a San Francisco Police Officer's regular rate of pay for the purpose of computing overtime pay due under this Agreement for the purpose of calculating maximum monthly wages in the wage setting survey process described in Article III, Section 1.B. except as set forth below in subsection (a) for Fiscal Year 2004-2005. Funding for this premium is anticipated from Federal Homeland Security grants. It is the parties' understanding that this benefit is part of the salary attached to all ranks and shall be included for purposes of retirement benefit calculations and contributions.

198. a. If Federal Homeland Security grant funding is obtained in fiscal year 2003-2004, the terrorism response training pay shall not be considered as part of a San Francisco Police Officer's regular rate of pay and shall be excluded from the wage setting survey process described in Article III, Section I.B. for fiscal year 2004-2005 only.
