109.1 Employees will receive a safety shoe allowance of $75 per year or $150 every other year.

109.2 In order to make the Evansville Division as safe and healthy a place to work as possible, Whirlpool may utilize safety studies to identify jobs that could lead to musculoskeletal disorder (heavy lifting, awkward posture, repetitive motion). Studies may be conducted on jobs that have resulted in injury or illness, that produce high turnover, or those that receive complaints by employees or the union. The safety department will be responsible for approving the studies and determining what if any actions are necessary to change the jobs. Possible actions to be taken include adding people, modifying work stations, replacing the job with automation, modifying parts, requesting the affected employee to bid to another job or to voluntarily transfer to another seniority division to prevent personal injury, and/or rotating jobs. If the employee moves by bid or by transfer, this "safety move" will not count against the normal bid and transfer limitations.
