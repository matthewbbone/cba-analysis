3. No employee shall be permitted to bid until such employee has passed his/her probationary period. If an eligible, qualified person fails to return the preliminary employment express interest in the job will be considered for such vacancy.

4. The senior employee who is unvoted on a job bid shall have up to five (5) days to qualify for each opening. After the employee is deemed qualified, a replacement bid will be posted. This five (5) day period may be extended by mutual agreement between the Company and the Union. If the employee has previously held the bid job within the last two (2) years, the five (5) day qualification period does not apply.

5. In this event such employee's seniority would return to his/her previous rank and shift.

6. The next senior person would be assigned from the original bid sheet and would begin on up to five (5) days to qualify.

7. When a successful bidder is awarded a job, then during the trial period elect to return to their previous jobs, he/she will be restricted from additional bidding for a period of four (4) months from the date the job was awarded. Employees shall not be permitted to bid in any other classification for four (4) months. Employee will be permitted to bid on a job with a higher rate of pay in a different classification.

8. If the vacancy is not filled under this provision outlined above, it will be offered to the senior qualified bidder on the seniority list.

9. As per Company policy, if a vacancy caused by quit, discharge, transfer, retirement or establishment of a new job is permanent and shall be further defined as a vacancy which will be posted for job bidding after all active displaced employees who hold "transfer" rights to this opening have first been solicited in seniority order.
